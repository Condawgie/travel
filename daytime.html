Welcome to Paris

The Louvre

Home to the Mona Lisa, The Louvre is the world's largest museum and a historic monument in Paris. It contains art from almost every civilization on earth. Located in the 1st arrondissemont of Paris.

Jardin Des Tuileries

Named after Queen Catherine de Medicis had built in 1564. The garden is located between the Louvre Museum. It is a place for walking amongst a beautiful french garden, verdant lawns, manicured rows of trees and gravel paths.

Saint Chappelle

Built by Louis IX (1214-1270), this gothic hewel is home to the oldest stained glass windows in Paris. It was created to house the kings collection of relics recovered from the impoverished emperor in Constantinople.

Norte Dame de Paris

Norte Dame is a medieval Catholic cathedral completed in 1345, on the Ile de la Cite in the 4th arrondissement. The cathedral is considered one of the finest examples of French Gothic architecture, and is among the most well-known church.

The Eiffel Tower

Named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel at the time the Eiffel tower was the largest man made structure in the world. Finished in 1889 as entrance to the 1889 World's Fair has become the most visited monument in the world.

Hotel Des Invalides

The Location of the remains of the French dictator Napoleon Bonaparte (1769-1821). Originally built in 1670 to house disabled soldiers. Napoleons tomb was sent here in 1840 after dying of exile.
