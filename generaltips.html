Shopping and Dining

Most places close between meal times and it is very rare to find open shops on Sundays.

Paris is a collection of arrondissements

The first arrondissement is in the dead center of Paris and the neighborhoods spiral outward from the arrondissement.

1st Arrondissement

The center of the city geographically and home to some of central Paris's top destinations; including the Louvre and the Tuileries.

Le Marais (3rd & 4th)

Today it is a ritzy yet funky neighborhood brimming with boutiques and parks. Here you can find Centre Pompidou and the Hotel de Ville.

Latin Quarter (5th)

The home to the Sorbonne and Pantheon. It is extremely popular with students and the streets are filled with young people.

Saint-Germain (6th)

Where you will find the former haunts of Camus, Sarte, and Hemingway. It is also filled with a wide variety of beautiful boutiques.

The 7th

Home to the Eiffel Tower. This is a ritzy residential neighborhood and there isn't much to do beyond seeing the tower and Les Invalides.

The 8th

Home to Champs Elysees and a shopping central. There is also the Arc de Triomphe and Place de la Concorde.

The 9th

Home to the Opera. I recommend people visit here to see a central neighborhood that isn't as touristy.

The 10th & 11th, as well as 19th & 20th

Considered the up-and-coming artsy area of Paris. Full of vibrant immigrant communities, art, edgy shops, and lower prices.

The 18th (Montmartre)

Home to the Sacre-Coeur, Moulin Rouge, and Place du Tertre. The 18th is a very lively place filled with exotic hops and fun.
